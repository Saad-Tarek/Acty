## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI-generated?** It passes the slop-*tell* test but not yet the brand-distinctiveness bar.

**LLM assessment**: No gradient text, no glassmorphism, no fake hero-metric template, no eyebrow-spam, real content photography, committed Fraunces/Inter type with genuine weight/scale contrast. It does not read as "an AI generated this." It does, however, read as a *competent Relume/Webflow template*: symmetrical centered section headers, an icon+heading+text service grid, recurring small kickers. For a `brand` register whose explicit anti-reference is "generic fitness-app SaaS," templated-but-clean is the real risk, not slop.

**Deterministic scan**: `detect.mjs` over `app/page.js`, `navbar-03`, `footer-03`, and all `components/ホーム/*` returned `[]` — zero findings. Clean. No side-stripe borders, gradient text, or eyebrow patterns tripped.

**Visual overlays**: Not injected; review based on Playwright screenshots at 1440/768/390 plus source.

## Overall Impression

A polished, on-brand, faithful reproduction of the comp with real photography and genuine accessibility work. It reads premium and warm. The single biggest opportunity is that the *faithful-repro* path leaves it looking like a well-made template rather than a distinctive brand surface — exactly the "base + elevate" tradeoff that was deferred at shape time.

## What's Working

1. **Photography carries the belonging story.** Real running/yoga/hiking/community images and believable testimonial portraits do the "show the people" job; the hero group-yoga shot lands the warmth in the first second.
2. **Typographic system is disciplined.** Fraunces display + Inter body with a real responsive scale; black body text on light schemes gives excellent (21:1) reading contrast, no muted-gray-on-tint failure.
3. **Accessibility is real, not cosmetic.** Visible focus rings, `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback, single page h1, valid heading nesting, descriptive JP alt text, aria labels.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] Hero body copy contrast over the bright image area.** The bottom-right paragraph is white over the sand/water region where the gradient overlay is weakest (~45%). Large headline is fine; the small body text likely dips under 4.5:1 there. *Why it matters:* fails the committed WCAG 2.2 AA target exactly where the value proposition is stated. *Fix:* add a localized bottom/right scrim or strengthen the gradient stop behind the paragraph. *Command:* `/impeccable polish`

- **[P1] Newsletter form has no states.** Submitting gives no loading, success, error, or inline validation; `type=email` is the only guard. *Why it matters:* the one real interaction on the page silently does nothing, undermining trust at a conversion point. *Fix:* inline validation, loading + success/error feedback, required handling. *Command:* `/impeccable harden`

- **[P2] Stats interactivity is undiscoverable.** The three stats are Radix tabs that swap the right-hand image, but they render as a static list with a 1px left rule and no obvious selected/hover affordance, so users won't know they're clickable. *Why it matters:* a built interaction nobody triggers is wasted, and the lone "play" image looks arbitrary. *Fix:* give the active stat a clear indicator (accent rule/weight), add hover affordance, or drop the tab behavior. *Command:* `/impeccable layout`

- **[P2] Templated composition vs. brand distinctiveness.** Centered headers, the symmetrical 3-card service grid, and recurring kickers read as category-default. Against PRODUCT.md's anti-reference and "energized, not loud / show the people" principles, this is the biggest brand lever. *Fix:* the deferred "base + elevate" pass — stronger hero type treatment, break one section's symmetry, art-direct the services or stats block. *Command:* `/impeccable bolder`

- **[P3] Recurring kicker labels.** 体験 / サービス / 実績 sit above three section headings. One named kicker is voice; three is approaching AI-grammar scaffolding. *Fix:* vary the cadence or drop them. *Command:* `/impeccable typeset`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer / hesitant wellness joiner)**: The hero CTAs 探索 / 参加 are verb-only and abstract ("Explore" / "Join" — join what, where?); a first-timer scanning for "is this for me / what do I do" gets two ambiguous buttons. The stats tabs won't be recognized as interactive. FAQ is reassuring and well-placed.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile)**: Layout stacks cleanly and images lazy-load, but the primary CTAs live at the top of the hero (out of the thumb zone), and the newsletter form gives no feedback on a flaky connection. Tap targets are adequate.

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent)**: Strong baseline — keyboard focus visible, reduced-motion honored, alt text present. Risks: hero body-copy contrast (above), and the stats tabs rely on the Radix roving-tabindex with weak visible selection, which is confusing under a screen reader paired with the faint visual state.

## Minor Observations

- Hero headline is English while the page is Japanese — a deliberate comp choice, reads as art direction, fine; just confirm it's intended brand voice.
- "詳細 / もっと見る" link pairs under features/services are low-information; the arrow link especially.
- Pure-black body text is maximal contrast but slightly harsh; a near-black (e.g. neutral-darker) would soften without losing AA. Optional.
- Stats' 1px left rule is a legitimate tab indicator (not a banned side-stripe), but it's doing double duty as decoration and affordance and succeeding at neither.

## Questions to Consider

- What would the *confident* version of this hero look like — could the type treatment alone (scale, a second weight, an oversized lockup) carry distinctiveness without adding noise?
- Do the stats need to be interactive at all? A single strong image plus three static figures may communicate more honestly.
- Is "Find your wellness community today" the line, or is there a sharper, more specific promise for a hesitant first-timer?
