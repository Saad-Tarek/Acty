# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Wellness community joiners: people in Japan who want to *belong* before they want to
work out. The draw is ongoing community (group chat, shared experiences, people who
show up week after week), with events as the hook that gets them in the door. They
arrive curious and a little hesitant, scanning for whether this feels like *their kind
of people*. Their context is mobile-first, often browsing in spare moments, deciding
whether to take the social risk of joining a first event (a run, a yoga session, a
meditation circle, a hike, a workshop).

The job to be done: "Help me find a wellness community I actually want to be part of,
and make showing up for the first time feel easy and welcome."

## Product Purpose

Acty is a premium wellness event platform that turns scattered solo wellness habits
into shared, recurring community life. It curates events across categories (running
clubs, yoga, meditation, hiking, fitness classes, workshops, community gatherings),
connects participants into an ongoing community, and offers a premium membership for
deeper access. The marketing surface exists to convert a curious visitor into a member
who books their first event and feels invited, not sold to.

Success looks like: a first-time visitor finishes the homepage feeling "these are my
people, I want in," and signs up or browses events without friction or hesitation.

## Brand Personality

**Warm, vibrant, grounded.** Friendly and energetic on the surface, with calm and
substance underneath. The voice is a welcoming friend who is genuinely glad you came,
not a coach barking reps and not a luxury concierge keeping you at arm's length.

- **Tone:** inviting, encouraging, human. Speaks *to* the reader ("come move with us"),
  not at them.
- **Emotional goal:** within the first few seconds, the visitor should feel energized
  and invited; warmth and belonging over prestige and exclusivity.
- **Bilingual JP/EN:** Japanese is first-class and so is English. Both are designed-for
  from the start (language switcher, no text baked into fixed widths, type and line
  length that hold up in CJK and Latin alike).

## Anti-references

- **Generic fitness-app SaaS.** No loud gradients, neon, gym-bro energy, hero-metric
  templates (big number + small label + gradient accent), or busy dashboard chrome on
  the marketing pages. Acty is a community you join, not a tracker you download.
- By extension: avoid the modal fitness-startup landing page. If the hero could belong
  to any workout app, restart.

## Design Principles

1. **Belonging before transaction.** Every section should answer "would I fit in here?"
   before it asks for a signup. Lead with people, community, and warmth; let the CTA
   follow naturally.
2. **Energized, not loud.** Convey movement and life through warmth, rhythm, and real
   imagery of people, not through aggressive color or animation. Vibrant ≠ noisy.
3. **Grounded premium.** The brand is premium because it's considered and calm, not
   because it's expensive-looking. Restraint with warmth, never cold minimalism.
4. **Show the people.** This is an image-led, human-centered brief. Real faces, real
   events, real community moments carry the design. Colored blocks where a photo of
   people belongs is a failure, not restraint.
5. **Bilingual by construction.** Treat JP and EN as equal citizens; layout, type
   sizing, and component copy must not assume one language's metrics.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **Target: WCAG 2.2 AA.** Body text ≥4.5:1 against its background (the system's
  near-black `--color-neutral-darkest` on light schemes supports this; watch any muted
  gray on tinted backgrounds). Large text ≥3:1.
- Visible, non-color-only focus states on all interactive elements; full keyboard
  navigability.
- Every animation needs a `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` alternative, including the
  marquee/loop animations already defined in the design system.
- Don't rely on color alone to convey meaning (the four chromatic palettes are
  accents, not the only signal).
- Bilingual content must keep tap targets and line lengths comfortable in both
  Japanese and English.
